Fascinating Marine Life

Blue Whales

Blue Whales

The blue whale is the largest animal known to have ever existed, reaching up to 100 feet in length.

Their heart is the size of a small car, and a child could swim through their arteries.

Octopus

Octopus

Octopus are highly intelligent creatures with complex nervous systems and three hearts.

They can change color, shape, and texture to blend with their surroundings.

Great White Sharks

Great White Sharks

Great white sharks can detect a single drop of blood in 25 gallons of water.

They have special electroreceptor organs called ampullae of Lorenzini that help them sense tiny electrical fields.

Explore Ocean Depths

Sunlight Zone (0-200m)

This is where most marine life thrives due to abundant sunlight.

Twilight Zone (200-1000m)

Very little light penetrates here. Home to many bioluminescent creatures.

Midnight Zone (1000-4000m)

No light reaches this depth. Strange adaptations help creatures survive.

Abyssal Zone (4000-6000m)

High pressure, cold temperatures, and complete darkness define this zone.

Hadal Zone (6000-11000m)

The deepest parts of the ocean, found in trenches and canyons.
